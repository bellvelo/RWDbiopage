what about me?

career to date

Prior to joining the CodeClan course, I have been a full time parent for the last 2 years. I decided to take a career break to both spend some time with my young children and also take some time to evaluate what I wanted to do next.

Before then I had spent 7 years working forMorgan Stanley in the equities trading department. This was an extremely fast paced role with great emphasis on problem solving, attention to detail and ability to work to multiple deadlines under great pressure.

Previous to that I worked for Pernod Ricard working within the export department. This was a role that required me to work with and coordinate multiple departments in the manufacturing process; with the aim to fulfilling affiliates orders on time. This role involved great organiation, problem solving and customer service skills.

cycling

Mountain biking was my gateway to Cycling (as an adult). We would spend days away at MTB centres in Scotland; mostly the 7 Stanes centres at Ae and Mabie Forrest. These rides would take the whole day by the time you travelled there and back. However once we started a family, that enevitably meant that spending all day Saturday away from the family wasn't really what I wanted to do (not on a regular basis anyway). It was partly due to this that I made the switch to Road Cycling, and I have never looked back. I love being on the bike. When I'm not out on a ride, I am thinking about the next time I will; constantly checking weather forecasts and organising friends to go out with. I use Strava to record my rides.

music

I am constantly listening to music, and am often ‘reprimanded’ for having all the radio’s on in the house at the same time. I love most types of music, but my specialist subject is ’third-division’ indie bands.

I go to as many gigs as time and money will allow. Recently I’ve been to see Field Music, The Lovely Eggs, Goat Girl, Dutch Uncles and Slug (twice) amongst others.

Every year the family all go to the Deershed festival in North Yorkshire. A fantastic family friendly 3 day festival that is serious about its music

CodeClan

CodeClan are the first digital skills academy in the UK to become a Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA) approved centre, which is recognised and endorsed by employers across Scotland. The intensive 16 week course is designed for career changers, with a view to turning them into job ready Junior Software Developers.

I have researched the course and have been home studying coding for the last 2 months. It is something that has really gotton under my skin. I love learning syntax principles and writing code which you can instantly see realised in your browser. Initaially I started with HTML and CSS, which I used to build this page.
